About Me

Ty Caulfield

I'm an Intuitive Abstract Artist with a focus on natural, up-cycled materials. I acquire used plywood destined for the landfill or burning. I put it on an easel, begin to outline it and let it tell me what it is and, how it should be painted. The wood shows me a vision of what its various components are saying. Sounds strange but that’s basically how it works. I paint with Felt Tipped Pens and Dry Erase Markers and, as such, I am limited in the colors available to me. As it turns out, that’s not such a bad deal. ROYGBIV with brown, gold and black. I don't have a set agenda when I begin. Everything just flows through me as I paint. Because I use plywood as the base and because the wood has been manipulated by humans, having both grain and scars, each piece tells a unique story that I try and tell. It's a small collaboration between nature and me. No two pieces of wood are the same. Hence none of my paintings are duplicates. Each is unique. This all began around 2015 (It might also have begun in the '60's when I was in college.) I dismantled a room I had built. The wood grains kinda called to me and I began drawing on them. It's been a passion now for almost a decade and I love doing it. It connects me to nature in a different way than I have been used to.
